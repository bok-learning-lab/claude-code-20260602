# Question-bank notes: common intro-chem confusions

Stand-in for the **corpus of student questions from prior years** (compiled from TFs).
These ground the simulator so it asks things real students actually ask, not generic
"tell me more" prompts. Organized by topic; the sample lesson uses the acid/base set.

---

## Acid/base & equilibria (matches the sample lesson)

**Strength vs. concentration**
- "Isn't a strong acid just a concentrated acid?" (the central conflation)
- "If I add water to a strong acid, does it turn into a weak acid?"
- "A strong acid always has a lower pH than a weak acid, right?"
- "Is 'strong' the same as 'dangerous' / 'corrosive'?"

**pH and neutrality**
- "Does pH 7 always mean it's safe / neutral?"
- "If I mix an acid and a base, do I always get pH 7? Don't they cancel?"
- "Why can a salt solution be acidic or basic — isn't salt neutral?"
- "Negative pH — is that even allowed?"

**The double arrow / equilibrium**
- "What does the <=> arrow actually mean? Does the reaction stop?"
- "At equilibrium are the forward and reverse reactions stopped, or still going?"
- "Does equilibrium mean equal amounts of reactant and product?"
- "When the equilibrium 'shifts,' what is physically moving?"

**K, Ka, and rate (a frequent tangle)**
- "Does a big K mean the reaction is fast?" (K vs. rate)
- "If I add more reactant, does K get bigger?" (K vs. Q; Le Chatelier)
- "Why is water left out of the equilibrium expression?"

**The math / method**
- "Why are we allowed to assume x is small? When is that NOT allowed?"
- "Where does the ICE table come from — is it just a trick?"
- "Why is pH a log? What's wrong with just using [H+]?"

## Other intro topics (for TFs teaching something else — swap in as needed)

**Limiting reagents**
- "Why isn't the limiting reagent just the one there's less of (by mass)?"
- "What happens to the leftover excess reagent — does it disappear?"
- "Why can't you just add up all the grams to get product mass?"

**Intermolecular forces**
- "Is hydrogen bonding a type of covalent bond?" (intra- vs. inter-molecular)
- "Why does boiling break IMFs but not the bonds inside the molecule?"
- "Bigger molecule = stronger bonds = higher boiling point, right?" (London dispersion oversimplified)

## How to use these
Hand the relevant block to the simulator as grounding. The persona should weave 1-3
of these in *naturally* and *in character* — Priya asserts them confidently, Marcus
asks them hesitantly, Dana fuses two of them into one wrong synthesis.
