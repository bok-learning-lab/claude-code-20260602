Student Simulator

Rehearse your microteaching mini-lesson against a simulated intro-chem student before you face a real one.

How it works

  1. Have your mini-lesson ready (outline or full plan).
  2. Pick a student persona below.
  3. Open the system prompt prompts/01-student-simulator.md, paste in your lesson and the chosen persona, set the intensity, and start teaching.
  4. The simulated student stays in character and questions you for ~6–8 exchanges. Type DEBRIEF when you're done.
  5. Run prompts/02-debrief.md for coaching feedback.
  6. Hand in: your saved chat transcript + your lesson plan.

Choose a student

Priya over-confident

Misconception: "strong acid" means concentrated / automatically lowest pH; conflates extent of dissociation with amount.

Sounds competent and agrees too fast — punishes a TF who races. Asserts wrong rules confidently.

Marcus anxious / careful

Misconception: pH 7 always = neutral/safe; acid + base always "cancel" to 7. Needs concrete mental models.

Slow, foundational, hedges. Exposes hidden assumptions — good for a first rehearsal.

Dana concept-confuser

Misconception: big K = fast reaction; equilibrium = "equal amounts"; mixes Le Chatelier shift with a change in K.

Builds plausible-but-wrong syntheses. Tests whether you catch the buried error.

Sam skeptic · advanced

Misconception: the ICE-table math is a meaningless ritual; "assume x is small" feels like cheating. Doubts why the topic matters.

Direct, math-comfortable, disengages at hand-waving. A harder run.
