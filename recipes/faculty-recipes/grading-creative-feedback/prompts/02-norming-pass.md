# Prompt template (optional): Norming / calibration pass

> Use this BEFORE grading a batch, early in the term. It mirrors the Day-1 move:
> "give it a few samples and ask what the common mistakes are" — so the rubric and
> my expectations are calibrated before any individual grade is drafted. This pass
> produces **no grades.**

---

You are helping me **calibrate** my rubric for grading peer feedback on creative
work, before I grade anyone. You will NOT assign grades in this pass.

**Read these inputs:**

- **Rubric for evaluating feedback:** {{feedback-rubric, e.g. context/feedback-rubric.md}}
- **Several student feedback samples** (anonymized): {{list of feedback files or a
  folder, e.g. several files like context/sample-student-feedback.md}}
- *(Optional)* **The creative works** the feedback responds to:
  {{creative-work files}}

**Do this:**

1. Read across all the samples as a set. Surface the **common strengths** — what
   several students already do well (e.g. "most quote specific lines").
2. Surface the **common weaknesses / recurring mistakes** — patterns I should
   watch for (e.g. "praise is specific but suggestions stay abstract").
3. Map each pattern to the rubric **criterion** it touches, and tell me where the
   rubric's point bands feel **too coarse, too generous, or ambiguous** given what
   you actually see in the samples.
4. Recommend any **rubric edits or clarifications** (reworded bands, an added
   example, a split criterion). Propose; do not apply.
5. Suggest **one or two anchor samples** I could use as reference points (e.g.
   "this one is a clear 'Proficient' on Actionability") — but label them as
   *candidates for me to confirm.*

**Hard rules:**

- **No grades, no point totals for individual students** in this pass. This is
  about the rubric, not the people.
- Quote from the samples as evidence for every pattern you name.
- Everything you propose is a **suggestion for me to approve or reject.** I own the
  rubric.

Begin your reply with: **"NORMING DRAFT — rubric calibration only, no grades."**
