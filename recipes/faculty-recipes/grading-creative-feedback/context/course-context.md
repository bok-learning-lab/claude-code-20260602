# Course context (syllabus excerpt)

**Course:** ENGL 142 — *Introduction to Creative Writing: Fiction & Poetry*
**Term:** Fall · **Meets:** Tue/Thu, workshop format

## The workshop and the feedback assignment

This is a studio workshop: every week, students submit short creative work (flash
fiction, poems, or short prose) and read two of their peers' pieces in advance.
Class time is spent in structured response. Learning to *give* good feedback is
treated as a core skill of the course — reading like a writer, naming what a
piece is trying to do, and helping a peer revise without rewriting it for them.

For each assigned piece, a student submits a **Peer Feedback Response** with two
parts: (1) brief intent/margin notes made while reading, and (2) an end-of-piece
summary comment addressed to the author. These are collected and graded for the
*quality of the feedback* — not for whether the reviewer "liked" the work.

## Weighting

Peer Feedback Responses are worth **20% of the final grade**, spread across the
semester (roughly one graded response every other week, lowest score dropped).
Each response is scored out of 20 points using the feedback rubric in
`feedback-rubric.md`. Because feedback is a craft skill the course is explicitly
teaching, the instructor norms the rubric early in the term (see the optional
norming-pass prompt) and gives written rationale alongside each grade so students
learn what strong feedback looks like.
