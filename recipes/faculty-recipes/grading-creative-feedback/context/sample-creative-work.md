**Title:** "The Last Good Light"

> *This is the original creative work. Student B's job was to give feedback on
> it; we grade Student B's feedback, not this piece.*

---

The lighthouse keeper kept a list of everything the sea had taken. Page one: his
father's boots. Page forty: a dog named Cartographer. Page two hundred and six,
the most recent entry, just three days old: *the good light.*

He wrote in pencil so the salt couldn't fade what the salt had already done.

Mornings, he climbed the one hundred and forty steps and polished a lamp that no
longer turned. The shipping lanes had moved east years ago; the boats that passed
now were pleasure craft with their own bright electronics, blinking past him like
people who don't see the old man at the bus stop. He polished the lamp anyway. A
clean dark thing is still a dark thing, but at least it is yours.

On the third night after the good light failed, a girl rowed out of the fog. She
could not have been more than twelve. She tied her boat to his and climbed the
one hundred and forty steps without asking, and stood at the dead lamp, and said,
"Show me how it worked."

He did not ask where her parents were. He did not ask how she knew. He only took
her small cold hand and placed it on the brass crank, and together they turned
the thing that would not turn, and turned it, and turned it.

In the morning the list had a new entry, page two hundred and seven, and for the
first time in years it was not a subtraction.

He did not write what it was. Some things you keep off the page.
