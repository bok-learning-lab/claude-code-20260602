# Rubric: Evaluating Peer Feedback on Creative Work

This rubric grades the **feedback a student gives**, not the creative work that
prompted it. Total: **20 points** across four criteria (5 points each). Use the
point bands below; partial points are allowed when feedback falls between bands.

A short label for each band: **Exemplary (5) · Proficient (4) · Developing
(2–3) · Minimal (0–1)**.

---

## 1. Specificity (5 pts)

Does the feedback point to *particular* moments, lines, or choices in the work
rather than offering vague, generic reactions?

- **5** — Repeatedly cites specific lines, images, or moments by name/quote.
- **4** — Mostly specific, with one or two general remarks.
- **2–3** — A mix of specific and vague; reader could not always locate the issue.
- **0–1** — Generic throughout ("I liked it," "the ending was weird").

## 2. Actionability (5 pts)

Could the writer actually *do something* with this feedback? Does it suggest
concrete revision directions rather than only describing reactions?

- **5** — Offers clear, doable revision moves the writer can act on.
- **4** — Mostly actionable; one or two suggestions left abstract.
- **2–3** — Names problems but rarely points toward a fix.
- **0–1** — No usable next steps; reaction only.

## 3. Evidence & Craft Awareness (5 pts)

Does the feedback show understanding of craft (e.g. imagery, pacing, point of
view, line breaks, dialogue) and support claims with evidence from the text?

- **5** — Uses craft vocabulary accurately and ties claims to textual evidence.
- **4** — Solid craft awareness; evidence mostly present.
- **2–3** — Some craft awareness but claims often unsupported.
- **0–1** — Personal taste only; no craft framework or evidence.

## 4. Tone & Generosity (5 pts)

Is the feedback constructive and respectful — honest about weaknesses while
recognizing what works and treating the writer as a capable peer?

- **5** — Warm, candid, balanced; names strengths and weaknesses generously.
- **4** — Constructive with minor lapses (slightly blunt or slightly thin praise).
- **2–3** — Either harsh/dismissive or so soft it avoids real critique.
- **0–1** — Unkind, dismissive, or purely flattering with no substance.
