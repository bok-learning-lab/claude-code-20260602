# Draft grade — Student B's feedback on "The Last Good Light"

**Course:** ENGL 142 · **Rubric:** `context/feedback-rubric.md` (20 pts)

## Per-criterion scores

### 1. Specificity — 5 / 5
Student B repeatedly anchors to specific moments rather than reacting generally.
The margin notes single out a line by quotation — *"'wrote in pencil so the salt
couldn't fade what the salt had already done' — say this twice out loud"* — and
the summary names the structural device precisely: *"Tracking loss by page number
… lets you measure grief without ever using the word."* The reader could locate
every observation in the text.

### 2. Actionability — 3 / 5
Uneven. The bus-stop note gives a concrete, doable move: *"Could the comparison
stay inside the story's own imagery — something coastal — so we don't leave the
island?"* But two key notes stop at reaction. On the ending, the advice is *"you
might trust the reader a hair more there"* — a direction, but not a move the
author can execute — and the opening praise offers no next step. Lands in the
**Developing/Proficient** boundary; scored at the top of Developing.

### 3. Evidence & Craft Awareness — 5 / 5
Strong, accurate craft vocabulary tied to evidence: identifies *"the list as a
structure,"* reads the *"closing reversal,"* and diagnoses the simile as an image
that breaks the story's world — *"the bus stop yanked me out of the sea world for
a beat."* Claims are consistently supported from the text.

### 4. Tone & Generosity — 5 / 5
Warm, candid, and balanced. Opens by naming what works (*"the list as a
structure"*), delivers critique without harshness, and treats the writer as
capable: *"I don't think you need to explain her … but a single concrete detail
… might let us commit to her being real if that's what you want."* Honest about
weaknesses, generous about strengths.

## For the instructor

- **Where reasonable graders might disagree:** Actionability. I read "trust the
  reader" as a *reaction* rather than an actionable move and scored a 3. If you
  count gentle directional nudges as actionable, this is a 4 and the total is 19.
- **Rubric ambiguity flagged:** the Actionability band doesn't say whether a
  *direction* ("trust the reader more") counts as actionable or whether it must be
  a *concrete revision move*. Worth clarifying during norming.

  piece, adjust the Actionability call, and assign the grade yourself.
